53.

Because the weight of the history book is the force exerted by Earth on the history book, we represent it as F→ EH=−14j^ N. Aside from this, the history book interacts only with the physics book. Because the acceleration of the history book is zero, the net force on it is zero by Newton’s second law: F→ PH+F→ EH=0→ , where F→ PH is the force exerted by the physics book on the history book. Thus, F→ PH=−F→ EH=−(−14j^ ) N=14j^ N. We find that the physics book exerts an upward force of magnitude 14 N on the history book. The physics book has three forces exerted on it: F→ EP due to Earth, F→ HP due to the history book, and F→ DP due to the desktop. Since the physics book weighs 18 N, F→ EP=−18j^ N. From Newton’s third law, F→ HP=−F→ PH, so F→ HP=−14j^ N. Newton’s second law applied to the physics book gives ∑F→ =0→ , or F→ DP+F→ EP+F→ HP=0→ , so F→ DP=−(−18j^ ) −(−14j^ ) =32j^ N. The desk exerts an upward force of 32 N on the physics book. To arrive at this solution, we apply Newton’s second law twice and Newton’s third law once.
